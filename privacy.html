Privacy Policy

Last updated: 28 April 2026

The short version: PDFtoLedger processes your bank statement PDF entirely in your browser. Your financial data never reaches our servers — we never see it, store it, or share it. This policy explains the limited data we do handle, such as your account email and billing details if you subscribe to a paid plan.

1. Who we are

PDFtoLedger is operated as a trading name in the United Kingdom. In this policy, "PDFtoLedger", "we", "us", and "our" refer to the operator of the website at pdftoledger.com.

For any privacy-related enquiries, you can contact us at: privacy@pdftoledger.com

2. What data we collect and why

Your bank statement PDF — we collect nothing. When you upload a PDF bank statement, it is processed entirely within your web browser using JavaScript. The file is never transmitted to our servers. We have no technical access to your financial data at any point.

Account information (paid plans only). If you sign up for a Starter or Pro plan, we collect:

  • Your email address — to identify your account and send you important service communications
  • Your billing name and country — as required for payment processing
  • Payment card details — handled entirely by Stripe, our payment processor. We never see or store your full card number

Usage data. We may collect limited, anonymised technical data to keep the service running reliably — for example, error logs that record what went wrong without recording the content of your file. This data does not identify you personally.

Communications. If you contact us by email, we retain that correspondence in order to respond to you and keep a record of your enquiry.

3. Cookies

PDFtoLedger does not currently use cookies for tracking or analytics. If you have a paid account, a session cookie is used solely to keep you logged in — it contains no personal or financial data and is deleted when you log out or close your browser.

We do not use third-party advertising cookies or tracking pixels.

4. Legal basis for processing (UK GDPR)

Where we process personal data, we do so on the following legal bases:

  • Contract — to provide the paid service you have signed up for, including billing and account management
  • Legitimate interests — to maintain the security and reliability of the service, and to respond to support enquiries
  • Legal obligation — where we are required to retain records for tax or regulatory compliance

5. How we share your data

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We share it only with the following third parties, and only to the extent necessary to operate the service:

  • Stripe — payment processing. Stripe is certified to PCI DSS Level 1 and processes your payment details under their own privacy policy. See stripe.com/gb/privacy.
  • Infrastructure providers — hosting and content delivery services used to run pdftoledger.com. These providers are contractually bound to process data only on our instructions.

We may disclose personal data if required to do so by law, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of PDFtoLedger, our users, or others.

6. How long we keep your data

  • Account data — retained for as long as your account is active, and for up to 6 years after closure for tax and accounting purposes, as required by UK law
  • Support correspondence — retained for 2 years after the enquiry is resolved
  • Error logs — retained for 90 days and then deleted automatically

Your bank statement file data is never stored on our servers at any point, so there is nothing for us to delete.

7. Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access — you can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — you can ask us to correct inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure — you can ask us to delete your data, subject to legal retention requirements
  • Right to restriction — you can ask us to limit how we use your data in certain circumstances
  • Right to portability — you can request your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to object — you can object to processing based on legitimate interests

To exercise any of these rights, email us at privacy@pdftoledger.com. We will respond within 30 days.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk or by calling 0303 123 1113.

8. Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect any personal data we hold. All data in transit is encrypted via HTTPS. Payment processing is handled exclusively by Stripe and never passes through our own infrastructure.

Because your bank statement PDF is processed locally in your browser, the greatest security risk — your financial data being intercepted or stored by a third party — does not apply to PDFtoLedger.

9. Children

PDFtoLedger is not directed at children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 16. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. If you have an account, we will notify you by email of any significant changes. Continued use of the service after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.

11. Contact us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or how we handle your data, please contact us:

  • Email: privacy@pdftoledger.com
  • Website: pdftoledger.com
